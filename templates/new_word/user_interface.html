Learn new words

The first question everyone asks is, how do you quickly memorize new words in a foreign language? Scientists believe that it is easier to memorize information when it has an emotional charge. Learning words while playing, thanks to rebuses or in films is therefore a good idea. If there's an English song that you love, feel free to look for the meanings of words you don't know. Pairing these words with a song you like will leave an emotional trail in your memory. The mnemonic is also a very valuable tool. By creating strong associations, you'll more easily remember the words you learn, even those that are difficult to pronounce. There are specialized sites where you can find different mnemonic techniques to help you memorize English words. However, the best way to learn is to create your own associations because these are unique to you and will be emotionally charged..

Using words in sentences

This is perhaps one of the most important concepts in this article: spaced repetition. There is no point in repeating a word 10 times in a row to learn vocabulary. It is better to do 10 repetitions (or reviews) spaced in time. Of course, the repetitions depend on each word and its level of difficulty. But this consists, for example, of revising after 10 minutes, then after 8 a.m., after 24 hours, after 3 days, 10 days, 25 days, etc. This technique allows you to store vocabulary in your long-term memory with very little time spent on it. There have been many studies on this. It is very important to use the frequency with which words are used when learning vocabulary. Indeed, what is the point of learning words and phrases that are not, or no longer, used? but sometimes present in school books?
